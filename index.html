Correlation between Vulnerable Populations and Expected Annual Loss of Natural Hazards in Colorado

Learn More

How at risk from natural hazards are vulnerable populations in Colorado?

With climate change, natural hazards are expeced to rise in frequency and magnitude.

Colorado, a state that has been hit several large natural hazards in the past decade, such as the 2013 Colorado floods and several wildfires, is at risk of many different natural hazards.

The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) has developed methods to calculate the expected annual loss of each county from different natural hazards.

With the rising risk of natural hazards, the topic of environmental justice comes to mind as vulnerable populations would be even more vulnerable.
- Environmental justice is a movement that seeks fair treatment for all people with respect of environmental issues.
- Vulnerable populations are groups that are of higher risk for poor health as a result of different factors such as: social, economic, or political.

Analysis for this project

To see whether there is a correlation between the expexted annual loss from different natural hazards and various vulnerable populations, an analysis using correlation statistics was done and is shown on the graph. The results of the analysis are values ranging from -1 to 1 meaning:

-1: Strong negative correlation. A county with a high expected annual loss would likely also have low vulnerable population values

0: No correlation.

1: Strong positive correlation. A county with a high expected annual loss would likely also have high vulnerable population values
